# Pattern: Recursive Improvement

**Ring:** 2 (Opt-in — mature collaborations)
**Compression source:** Forge's own post-forge debrief + meta-audit cycles

---

## What It Is

The hub periodically reviews and improves its own operating agreements:
- **Health checks** at session milestones (20, 50, 100)
- **Pattern evaluation** — what's working, what's overhead, what's missing
- **Agreement updates** — CLAUDE.md, CONSTITUTION.md, skills evolve
- **Debrief capture** — structured reflection feeds pattern evolution

---

## Why It Matters

Operating agreements that never change become stale or constraining:
- Initial choices may not fit after 50 sessions
- Pain points accumulate without surfacing
- The collaboration outgrows its scaffolding
- "We've always done it this way" ossifies

Recursive improvement applies CI to the collaboration's own infrastructure.

---

## When to Use

**After sufficient operational history:**
- 50+ sessions, OR
- Explicit request for self-improvement capability, OR
- CI triage reveals structural issues (not just tactical fixes)

**Interview gate:** "Would you want the collaboration to periodically review and update its own operating agreements?"

---

## Components

### Session Milestone Health Checks

At session 20, 50, 100 (then every 50):

**Quick scan (session 20):**
> "Are the patterns working? Any friction with BOS/EOS/CI? Anything missing?"

3-5 questions, lightweight. Generate suggestions, don't auto-file.

**Deeper review (session 50, 100):**
> "Should any patterns be added, removed, or modified? Has the collaboration's needs changed? Are operating agreements still accurate?"

May result in:
- Pattern activation (Ring 2 → active)
- Pattern deactivation (overhead > value)
- Agreement amendments
- Skill updates

### Post-Session Debrief (Optional)

After significant sessions, brief reflection:

1. "Were there questions I should have asked but didn't?"
2. "Did any part of the workflow feel awkward?"
3. "Where was I uncertain about recommendations?"

Feeds into next health check.

### Trigger-Based Review

When CI triage suggests structural problems (not just tactical), flag for next health check:

> "This issue suggests a pattern gap, not a one-off fix. Flagged for session 50 review."

## Artifacts Generated

### If pattern selected
- Health check milestones in BOS skill (20, 50, 100)
- Debrief prompt template
- `IMPROVEMENTS_LOG.md` — captures evolution history
- Pattern evaluation framework in health check

---

## Interview Questions

> "How long have you been working with AI in a structured way?"

Longer experience → may be ready for recursive improvement.

> "Do you have existing practices for improving how you work?"

Yes → recursive improvement fits naturally.

> "Would you want the collaboration to periodically review and update its own operating agreements?"

Direct gate.

---

## Warnings

**Don't over-meta.** The goal is effective collaboration, not endless self-examination. If health checks consume significant session time, reduce frequency.

**Trust the early choices.** Session 20 health check should mostly confirm that Ring 1 patterns are working. Major changes at session 20 suggest the initial interview missed something.

**Structural changes need bilateral agreement.** Operating agreements are bilateral. Unilateral changes — even improvements — violate the collaboration's consent structure.

**Recursive improvement is recursive.** At session 100+, you can evaluate whether recursive improvement itself is valuable. If not, turn it off.

---

## Orienting Note

*This pattern emerged from Forge's own development: each Forge run produces a debrief, which updates SKILL.md, which improves the next run. The recursion compounds. But it requires enough operational history to have something to evaluate. If you're at session 10, you don't have enough data. Trust the initial setup. The recursive layer activates when you're ready.*
